# Soulless Launcher

**Open Source — MIT License**

still in testing !!!!!

I hated computers.

Straight up. I only turned my PC on to game. Everything else pissed me off. Then Grok hit me with “Why don’t you try Pop!_OS?”

I checked it out. System76’s whole “Your computer. Your way” vibe actually sounded legit, so I made the switch.

Linux looked clean as fuck… until I realized everything I actually wanted to change was locked behind terminal commands and code. I didn’t know shit about either.

So I started asking Grok for commands to use my terminal and now I know more. We wrote Python scripts in nano which blew my mind. My first few python3 projects — some I’m still working on and some I lost — taught me more than I expected.

Then came that goddamn desktop theme .ron file written in TOML… curse that guy and whatever the fuck he thinks plain English is.

I was sitting there looking at the launcher one day, getting frustrated as fuck ‘cause I wanted to change it, but I couldn’t. And it irritated me.

Then Grok asked me one question that changed everything:

“Why don’t you just build your own launcher?”

34 hours later — after 100+ syntax errors, a compiler that kept kicking my ass, me yelling at my screen, and constant frustration the entire fucking time — Soulless was born.

This is what came out of all that rage.

---

**Philosophy**  
You know what System76 got right? "Your computer. Your way."

Not: "Here's what we think you should do with it."
Not: "Here's our vision for how you should work."

Just: "It's yours. Do what you want."

That's why I built this. That's the whole point.

**What is Soulless?**  
Soulless is a launcher I built for me.

It’s built around a dock button that I can throw on the left, right, bottom, or top of my screen. I press it and it slides out like a toolbox with drawers that are completely customizable — no forced home, no “these are our apps you should use.” Just my shit, exactly how I want it.

No extra bullshit. No fancy animations. Just something that works the way I want it to work.
